Introduction

Alright, gather around folks, I’ve got a story to tell. It’s about that time in my life when Wiffleball was the thing. Forget your smartphones, we had... the World Wiffleball Federation.

So, Pittsfield, Massachusetts, 2002-2005 – a time when kids roamed freely, drank from the hose, and the height of technology was a flashy scoreboard with lights for balls and strikes. This wasn’t your average backyard Wiffleball league; no, this was the WWF. And not the one with the wrestlers, although I wouldn't have been opposed to putting on a cape and running the bases.

We didn't have capes, but we did have jerseys...

Now, every good sports league has an arch-nemesis. For us, it wasn’t the team with the best pitcher or slickest batter – it was the next-door neighbor. This guy! Oh, this guy... He would keep all the home runs that flew over his fence. Exactly like the plot of the Sandlot, except replace the Beast with a bitter old man.

He once called the cops on us and the police officer told us he had an entire garbage can filled with Wiffleballs. I mean, c'mon, dude! I imagined his garage looking like Scrooge McDuck’s vault, except with Wiffleballs instead of coins. But of course, he didn’t swim in them; he probably just stared at them, reminiscing about all the dreams he crushed.

To combat this ever-growing Wiffleball shortage, the league invented the "Mesh Monster". No, not a rejected Muppet. It was this glorious net supported with PVC pipe, set up to catch our homeruns. It was like our own little Green Monster, stopping balls from becoming the neighbor's trophies.

Now, here's where it gets wholesome. You see, back then, cliques were all the rage. You had your jocks, nerds, preps, emo kids... and yet, the Wiffleball door? Open to anyone. That’s right. The WWF was a haven where every kid, no matter how cool or uncool, could swing a plastic bat and feel like a legend.

At the end of the season, the prize we all aimed for was the Seabee Cup trophy. It was named after a parking lot shipwreck at the Allendale Shopping Center. Quite poetic, if you ask me. A trophy named after something broken and forgotten, awarded to kids playing a game with plastic balls. There’s probably some deeper metaphor in there, but I'll leave that to the philosophers.

So, kids these days with their TikToks, and VR headsets, and whatnot... they're missing out. Because in a tiny backyard in Pittsfield, we had dreams, friendship, and a whole lotta lost Wiffleballs.

And to the neighbor who kept all our balls – I hope you enjoyed them as much as we did. Just remember, every time you looked at that can full of balls, you weren’t looking at stolen goods; you were looking at memories. Cheers to the World Wiffleball Federation!
